About the Workshop

Welcome

The 2026 Emerging Leaders Workshop: Changing the Face of Cancer Center Leadership equips developing cancer center leaders with a foundation in leadership within the specific context of matrix comprehensive cancer centers.

The workshop curriculum is designed with content and experiences to cultivate leadership excellence and a deep understanding of organizational structure and strategy. Participants will explore what it means to lead within a complex, matrixed institution — navigating shared authority, interdisciplinary collaboration, and institutional mission.

In addition to skill-building, the workshop offers strengths identification through the CliftonStrengths Assessment, unique networking and team-building opportunities, and concrete strategies for career advancement within NCI-designated cancer centers.

This workshop is a partnership between the University of New Mexico Comprehensive Cancer Center and USC Norris Comprehensive Cancer Center, two NCI-designated institutions committed to developing the next generation of diverse cancer center leaders.

Leaders

Featured Speakers

CL
Caryn Lerman, PhD
Director, USC Norris Comprehensive Cancer Center Associate Dean for Cancer Programs H. Leslie and Elaine S. Hoffman Chair in Cancer Research USC Distinguished Professor, Keck School of Medicine

Dr. Caryn Lerman is nationally recognized for her cancer prevention research that bridges the fields of neuroscience, genomics, pharmacology, and behavioral science. She has published over 380 peer-reviewed articles throughout her distinguished career.

An elected member of the National Academy of Medicine, Dr. Lerman has served as President of the Association of American Cancer Institutes, member of the NCI Board of Scientific Advisors, the National Human Genome Research Advisory Council, and the National Institute on Drug Abuse Advisory Council.

Dr. Lerman is the former chair of the NCI Cancer Center Review Committee, where she played a pivotal role in evaluating and supporting NCI-designated cancer centers across the country.

Cancer Prevention Neuroscience Genomics National Academy of Medicine
YS
Yolanda Sanchez, PhD
Director and CEO, The University of New Mexico Comprehensive Cancer Center The Maurice and Marguerite Liberman Distinguished Chair in Cancer Research Professor, Department of Internal Medicine

Dr. Yolanda (Yoli) Sanchez is the first Hispanic/Latin woman to lead a National Cancer Institute-designated Comprehensive Cancer Center — a historic milestone for representation in cancer center leadership. She is an international expert in DNA damage response pathways.

Prior to joining the University of New Mexico, Dr. Sanchez served as the Associate Director of Basic Sciences at Dartmouth Cancer Center, another NCI-designated Comprehensive Cancer Center.

As Director and CEO of the UNM Comprehensive Cancer Center (UNMCCC), Dr. Sanchez is responsible for all cancer research and clinical programs at the University of New Mexico, spanning all schools, colleges, departments, and facilities. She collaborates with leadership at UNM, The Health Sciences, and UNM Health, as well as New Mexico's legislative and executive branches to bring the latest cancer prevention diagnostics and therapeutics to New Mexicans.

DNA Damage Response Cancer Center Leadership Historic First
CH
Chanita Hughes Halbert, PhD
Executive Vice Chair, Vice Chair for Research & Distinguished Professor, Department of Population and Public Health Sciences Dr. Arthur and Priscilla Ulene Chair in Women's Cancer, Keck School of Medicine Associate Director for Cancer Health Disparities, USC Norris Comprehensive Cancer Center

Dr. Chanita Hughes Halbert is renowned for her leadership in cancer prevention and minority health research. She has dedicated her career to reducing disparities in cancer outcomes that affect patients from underrepresented communities, with a primary focus on African American communities.

She has identified sociocultural, psychological, genetic, and environmental determinants of cancer health disparities, and translates this knowledge into evidence-based interventions to improve health equity among racially and ethnically diverse populations, as well as other medically underserved groups.

Hughes Halbert was elected to the National Academy of Medicine in 2017. She received the American Cancer Society's Cancer Control Award in 2010. President Barack Obama appointed her to the National Cancer Institute's Board of Scientific Advisors in 2012, and in 2014 she joined the National Advisory Council of the National Human Genome Research Institute.

Health Equity Cancer Disparities National Academy of Medicine
DC
David Chow, MA, MS, PCC
Leadership Development Executive Coach Founder, Leading Together & Trusted Advisor Associates, LLC

In 2004, David (Dave) Chow founded Leading Together, a company that provides executive coaching, leadership development, and organizational consulting. His passion is to inspire and challenge leaders toward personal mastery and psychological well-being, resulting in thriving organizations and, ultimately, a better world.

A highly effective and seasoned executive coach, David has coached hundreds of leaders from the C-Suite to emerging leaders across diverse industries — including high-tech, biotech, healthcare, engineering, construction, research/academia, government, and non-profit organizations.

Described as a creative, energetic, and highly engaging facilitator, David has designed and facilitated custom leadership programs across the globe on topics including Authentic Leadership, Leading with Emotional Intelligence, Executive Presence, and Strengths-Based Leadership.
