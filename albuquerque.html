Your ABQ Guide

Must-Sees & Must-Dos

🍽️ Dining & Food Scene
Workshop Suggestion
Sawmill Market
A vibrant food hall in the Sawmill District with an ever-rotating roster of local vendors, craft beverages, and New Mexican bites. Perfect for a casual evening exploring with new colleagues.
📍 1909 Bellamah Ave NW
Workshop Suggestion
Seasons Rotisserie & Grill
An Albuquerque classic with a gorgeous rooftop patio overlooking Old Town. Known for rotisserie chicken, New Mexican flavors, and an outstanding wine list — great for a group dinner.
📍 2031 Mountain Rd NW
Workshop Dinner
Campo at Los Poblanos
Your Day 1 workshop dinner venue — and one of ABQ's most beloved farm-to-table restaurants. Nestled on a historic lavender farm with locally sourced, seasonal New Mexican cuisine.
📍 4803 Rio Grande Blvd NW
Local Favorite
Duran's New Mexican Restaurant
A beloved neighborhood institution serving authentic New Mexican food since 1929. Warm, unpretentious, and the place locals send their out-of-town guests for red or green chile.
📍 1800 Central Ave NW
Don't Miss
Casa de Benavidez
A family-owned New Mexican gem that's been serving the community for decades. Big portions, award-winning green chile, and a warm hacienda atmosphere that feels quintessentially ABQ.
📍 8032 Fourth St NW
Brunch Pick
Frontier Restaurant
An Albuquerque legend right across from UNM campus. Open since 1971, famous for breakfast burritos, green chile stew, and their legendary sweet rolls. Cash-only, always worth it.
📍 2400 Central Ave SE
🎨 Culture & Arts
Must See
Old Town Albuquerque
Founded in 1706, Albuquerque's historic heart is a walkable plaza surrounded by adobe buildings, galleries, jewelry shops, and the stunning San Felipe de Neri Church. Plan at least an hour here.
📍 Old Town Plaza · 5 min from Hotel Chaco
Cultural Gem
Indian Pueblo Cultural Center
A world-class museum and cultural center operated by New Mexico's 19 Pueblos. Stunning exhibits on Indigenous history, art, and traditions — a profound and moving experience.
📍 2401 12th St NW · Walking from Hotel Chaco
Art Scene
516 Arts
Downtown ABQ's leading contemporary art center, showcasing local, national, and international artists with rotating exhibitions. Free admission and always thought-provoking.
📍 516 Central Ave SW
🏔️ Outdoors & Nature
Iconic ABQ
Sandia Peak Tramway
The world's longest aerial tramway whisks you 10,378 feet above sea level with panoramic views of the Rio Grande valley and the vast New Mexican landscape. Breathtaking at sunset.
📍 30 Tramway Rd NE
Only in ABQ
Petroglyph National Monument
Over 24,000 ancient rock carvings made by Ancestral Puebloans and early Spanish settlers line this volcanic escarpment. An awe-inspiring reminder of the deep history of this land.
📍 6001 Unser Blvd NW · Free Entry
Morning Walk
Rio Grande Nature Center
A peaceful riparian sanctuary along the Rio Grande, minutes from Hotel Chaco. Excellent birdwatching, scenic riverside trails, and a genuine sense of calm before the workshop day begins.
📍 2901 Candelaria Rd NW
☕ Coffee & Quick Bites
Local Roaster
Zendo Coffee
Albuquerque's beloved specialty coffee roaster, with beautiful shop spaces and some of the best single-origin coffee in the Southwest. A calm spot for early morning prep before shuttle pickup.
📍 Multiple locations downtown
ABQ Staple
Flying Star Cafe
A beloved local chain with fantastic baked goods, strong coffee, and hearty breakfast/lunch options. Relaxed, welcoming atmosphere — great for an informal meeting or a solo working session.
📍 Several ABQ locations
